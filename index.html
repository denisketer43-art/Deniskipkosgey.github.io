Getting to Know Me

About Me

I am young Kenyan who is deeply interested in how ideas, people, and institutions can come together to solve real challenges in society. My work and experience have grown from a curiosity about development, governance, and community well being, and a desire to be part of solutions that make systems work better for people.

Currently, I serve as a Strategy and Resource Mobilization Intern at Maasai Mara University under the Public Service Commission of Kenya, where I support strategic planning, proposal development, partnership building, and project monitoring. I previously gained experience in trade policy and analysis at the Ministry of Industry, Trade and Investment, and developed strong customer service and operations skills in the retail sector at Umoja Shoe Co. Ltd.

Beyond work, I am committed to community service through volunteering with the Kenya Red Cross, supporting outreach and resilience initiatives. I am especially passionate about inclusion and amplifying the voices of marginalized and indigenous communities.

My goal is to connect policy, people, and practical solutions to create meaningful and lasting impact.

Academic Background

Education

Bachelor's Degree in International Relations and Diplomacy

I hold a Bachelor's degree in International Relations and Diplomacy from Pioneer International University. My studies gave me a strong understanding of international relations, foreign policy, governance, diplomacy, political systems, and development. This academic journey helped me understand how institutions operate and how decisions at different levels influence societies.

As I progressed in my studies, I specialized in Peace and Security Studies, focusing more deeply on issues such as conflict prevention, peacebuilding, security dynamics, and community and national stability. This specialization added focus to my studies and helped me better understand the practical realities of maintaining peace in different contexts.

Overall, my university journey shaped how I think and approach problems. It strengthened my research and analytical skills and deepened my interest in research, public service, governance, and contributing to solutions that support peaceful and sustainable development.

Professional Journey

Experience

Public Service Commission of Kenya (PSCK) – Maasai Mara University

Strategy and Resource Mobilization Intern

I am Strategy and Resource Mobilization Intern in Maasai Mara University under the Public Service Commission. In my current role, I support the development and implementation of the university's strategic plans and performance tracking frameworks. I am involved in identifying funding opportunities and contributing to the preparation of concept notes and grant proposals. I also conduct research and data analysis to guide institutional planning and decision making.

Beyond research, I coordinate stakeholder engagement activities, partnerships, and donor relations to strengthen collaboration between the university and external partners. I assist in preparing reports, briefs, and presentations on resource mobilization progress and project outcomes. Additionally, I participate in monitoring and evaluation activities to ensure that funded projects align with institutional goals and deliver intended impact.

Umoja Shoe Co. Ltd.

Retail Operations

I worked closely with customers to ensure a positive shopping experience, providing support, guidance, and efficient service, which contributed to increased customer satisfaction and repeat visits. I handled transactions, returns, and inventory tracking using POS systems while ensuring accuracy in daily sales operations.

I also supported merchandising and in-store promotions, helping maintain an organized and attractive retail environment. My responsibilities included receiving incoming shipments, unloading delivery trucks, and assisting in stock management. I frequently assisted customers with product inquiries, promotions, and special offers, ensuring they had a smooth and informed shopping experience.

Ministry of Industry, Trade and Investment (State Department of Trade)

International Trade Division – Intern

During my attachment, I participated in trade related research and policy support activities within the International Trade Division. I assisted in the preparation of country position papers for trade negotiations and contributed to the analysis of trade data using statistical tools to support policy insights.

I also supported the dissemination of international trade information to local business communities, helping improve awareness of trade opportunities. In addition, I attended bilateral trade engagements and contributed to discussions on trade policy, gaining firsthand exposure to international trade processes and diplomacy in practice.

Kenya Red Cross Society

Volunteer

I have been actively involved in community outreach and resilience-building initiatives focused on supporting vulnerable populations. My work included assisting in emergency response activities and participating in community service programmes aimed at improving welfare and preparedness.
